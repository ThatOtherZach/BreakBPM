# BreakBPM

**The score tracker built for people who actually play fast.**

Most pool nights have a moment where nobody remembers who sank what, how long the game took, or who was genuinely on fire. BreakBPM fixes that.

## What it does

BreakBPM tracks every ball, every shot, and your live **Balls Per Minute**, a single number that tells you exactly how hot your streak is every game. The higher the BPM, the faster you're running the table.

BPM is calculated per-player, anchored at your first pocketed ball of the game, so it reflects *your* pace. Every shot is logged and stacked to help you improve your game.

Speaking of games, here's the modes:

- **8-Ball**: Solids vs Stripes. Basic rules including Golden Break and foul-on-8.
- **9-Ball**: Sink the balls in order and win with the 9. That's it.
- **Practice**: Solo drills with unlimited racks. Play how you want and log each shot.
- **🦈 Shark Mode (Solo 8-Ball)**: Play classic 8-ball against an invisible pool Shark. See below.

## Play anywhere, with anyone

Every game has a **5-character share code**. The host's device is always the scorekeeper; one phone runs the rack while everyone else follows along live.

**Join a game** by entering the code (or opening a `breakbpm.com/join/CODE` link). If you grab one of the open seats before the break, you're in the player roster with your name, group, and live BPM (no account required), guests are welcome.

If you join a game after the first pocketed ball, you'll be a spectator. Once the game's underway or all players are loaded (4 player max), you'll be a spectator instead. Spectators are view-only and can leave at any time.

**Spectate by name** at `breakbpm.com/watch/USERNAME` to follow a player's live game without the code; handy for railbirds. Spectators can hop in any time, watch the full HUD, shot log, and BPM, and don't take up a seat.

Note that spectators can join for free, but the host player will need an active pass or subscription for their games to be watchable.

**Sign in** to unlock full game history, and persistent stats. Your username is automatically set for Player 1; no need to type it each time! Lifetime pass holders can also set a custom username.

## Why BPM?

Because most pool nights are a mess. Different bar rules, people forgetting what they’re shooting, slow play, arguments about whether it was a foul or not.

After a decade of dealing with all of it, I built an app I actually wanted: one that strips everything unnecessary away.

BreakBPM doesn’t try to simulate the table or read the balls with AI vision mumbo jumbo. It just logs your shots, one ball at a time, and gives you two things back:

- Accuracy for what actually matters
- BPM is a number you can compare with your friends

The higher the BPM, the faster the game moves. That’s it. No fake science. Just something fun to chase while you’re actually playing.

The app is basically a remote for the table. Simple. Fast. No bullsh!t.

## 🦈 Shark Mode

You play solo against an invisible pool shark. Your first sink locks in your group (solids or stripes); the other goes to the Shark. Clear your group and sink the 8 to win, but every miss or foul gives the Shark a chance to pocket a ball.

**Clearing the Shark's ball off the real table.** Shark Mode is an honor system, when the Shark "pockets" a ball, you decide which ball left on the table is gone and physically remove it. There are two ways to handle it:

- **Pick the easy one:** Look at the Shark's remaining balls, choose whichever looks like an easy shot, and remove it from the table.
- **Shoot it for them:** Line up one of the Shark's balls and try to sink it yourself, playing the shot *as the Shark*. If you miss, remove it anyway and play through.

Either way, the ball comes off the table. Tap the Shark's ball in the selector to record it so the rack on your screen matches the felt in front of you.

**Shark Mode Rules:**

- **Sink a ball** → It counts for you.
- **Safety** → Never triggers a pocketed ball for the shark. It's a valid tactical play.
- **Only the 8 left + you miss or foul** → The Shark wins.
- **Sink the 8 after clearing your group** → You beat the Shark.

**Shark Aggression:**

- **Normal** The Shark pockets only on a foul.
- **Hard** The Shark pockets on every miss *and* every foul.
