# BreakBPM

**The score tracker built for people who actually play fast.**

Most pool nights end with nobody knowing who sank what, how long the game took, or who was genuinely on fire. BreakBPM fixes that.

## What it does

BreakBPM tracks every ball, every shot, and your live **Balls Per Minute** — a single number that tells you exactly how hot your streak is. The higher the BPM, the faster you're running the table.

BPM is calculated per-player, anchored at your first pocket of the game, so it reflects *your* pace — not a table-wide average. Every pocketing shot is stamped with your BPM at that moment so you can trace your pace shot by shot.

- **8-Ball** — Solids vs Stripes. Full rules including Golden Break and foul-on-8.
- **9-Ball** — Sink the 9 to win. That's it.
- **Practice** — Solo drills with unlimited racks. Watch your BPM improve session over session.
- **<img src="/shark-icon.png" alt="" class="about-shark-icon" /> Shark (1P 8-Ball)** — Solo 8-ball vs an invisible Shark opponent. See below.

## Why BPM?

Pool isn't just about who wins — it's about *how* you play. A 12 BPM game against a 6 BPM opponent tells a different story than the scoreboard alone. BreakBPM gives casual players a stat that actually means something at the table.

## Features at a glance

- Live per-player BPM + game timer
- HUD shows balls remaining for your group (including the 8), or "8-BALL TO WIN" once your group is cleared
- Per-shot BPM stamped in the shot log — trace your pace over the whole game

## <img src="/shark-icon.png" alt="" class="about-shark-icon about-shark-icon-lg" /> Shark Mode

Pick **8-Ball** and set the player count to **1** — Shark Mode kicks in automatically.

You're playing solo against an invisible opponent. Your first sink locks in your group (solids or stripes); the other goes to the Shark. Clear your group and sink the 8 to win — but every miss gives the Shark a chance to steal a ball.

**The rules:**

- **Sink a ball** → it counts for you.
- **Miss** → the Shark steals a random ball from your group (never the 8 while others remain).
- **Foul** → no steal on Normal aggression. On Hard, the Shark steals here too.
- **Safety** → never triggers a steal. It's a valid tactical play.
- **Only the 8 left + you miss or foul** → Shark wins outright.
- **Sink the 8 after clearing your group** → you win.

**Aggression toggle (set during setup):**

- **Normal** — Shark steals only on a clean miss.
- **Hard** — Shark steals on every miss *and* every foul.
